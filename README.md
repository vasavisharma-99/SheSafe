
# SheSafe

## Safety-Aware Navigation and Public Transport Assistant for Women

### Overview

SheSafe is a safety-first navigation platform designed to help women make informed travel decisions, especially during late hours and while using public transportation.

Existing navigation applications primarily optimize routes based on time, distance, and cost. However, these factors do not always reflect the safety of a route, bus stop, waiting area, or drop-off location.

SheSafe introduces safety as a key decision parameter by evaluating multiple travel options and providing users with a comparative safety score alongside travel time and cost estimates.

---

## Problem Statement

Women travelling during late hours often face uncertainty when choosing routes to reach their destination safely. While applications such as Google Maps, public transport apps, and cab services help users navigate efficiently, they do not provide sufficient information regarding route safety.

As a result, users may unknowingly choose routes that involve poorly lit roads, isolated bus stops, low crowd density, long walking distances, or unsafe waiting areas.

The goal of SheSafe is to empower women with safety-aware travel recommendations that help them choose not only the fastest or cheapest route, but also the safest one.

---

## Proposed Solution

SheSafe provides:

* Route comparison based on safety, time, and cost
* Safety score for each route
* Community-driven safety insights
* Emergency assistance features
* Safe travel recommendations for late-night journeys

The platform aims to help users make better travel decisions before entering potentially unsafe situations.

---

## Current Prototype Features

### User Registration

Users can create a profile and add emergency contact information.

### Route Planning

Users enter their source and destination to view available travel options.

### Route Comparison

Routes are compared based on:

* Estimated travel time
* Estimated cost
* Safety score

### Safety Score System

A rule-based safety model evaluates routes using factors such as:

* Crowd presence
* Lighting conditions
* Walking distance
* Public transport accessibility
* Community feedback
